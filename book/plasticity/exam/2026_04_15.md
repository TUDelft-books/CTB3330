:::::{exercise}
:label: exam_1_2
:nonumber: true

Determine the collapse load $q$ for the given structure using collapse mechanisms. Show the governing collapse mechanism.

:::::

::::{admonition} Uitwerking
:class: solution, dropdown

There are three potential collapse mechanisms, the first one:

```{figure} ./2026_04_15_data/mech_1.svg
:align: center
:source: https://github.com/Structural-Mechanics-CEG/mechanics-figures-source/tree/main/exam_plasticity
:number:
```

From the shape of the mechanism it follows that:

- $\delta w_{\rm{S}_2} = 3\cdot \delta \varphi_{\rm{S}_2 \rm{C}}$
- $\delta w_q = 1.5 \cdot \delta \varphi_{\rm{S}_2 \rm{C}}$

Applying the principle of virtual work gives:

$$
\begin{align*}
\delta A &= 0 \\
q \cdot 4 \cdot 1.5 \cdot \delta \varphi_{\rm{S}_2 \rm{C}} - 108 \cdot \delta \varphi_{\rm{S}_2 \rm{C}} &= 0 \\
q &= \frac{108}{6} = 18 \, \rm{kN/m}
\end{align*}
$$

The second mechanism is:

```{figure} ./2026_04_15_data/mech_2.svg
:align: center
:source: https://github.com/Structural-Mechanics-CEG/mechanics-figures-source/tree/main/exam_plasticity
:number:
```

From the shape of the mechanism it follows that:

- $\delta w_{q_2} = \delta \varphi_{\rm{S}_2 \rm{S}_3}$
- $\delta_{w_{\rm{S}_3}} = 2 \cdot \delta \varphi_{\rm{S}_2 \rm{S}_3}$
- $\delta_{w_{q_1}} = \delta \varphi_{\rm{S}_2 \rm{S}_3}$
- $\delta \varphi_{\rm{S}_1 \rm{S}_2} = \delta \varphi_{\rm{S}_2 \rm{S}_3}$

Applying the principle of virtual work gives:

$$
\begin{align*}
\delta A &= 0 \\
q \cdot 2 \cdot \delta \varphi_{\rm{S}_2 \rm{S}_3} - 108 \cdot \delta \varphi_{\rm{S}_2 \rm{S}_3} - 108 \cdot \delta \varphi_{\rm{S}_2 \rm{S}_3} + q \cdot 2 \cdot \delta \varphi_{\rm{S}_2 \rm{S}_3} &= 0 \\
q &= \frac{216}{4} = 54 \, \rm{kN/m}
\end{align*}
$$

The third mechanism is:

```{figure} ./2026_04_15_data/mech_3.svg
:align: center
:source: https://github.com/Structural-Mechanics-CEG/mechanics-figures-source/tree/main/exam_plasticity
:number:
```

From the shape of the mechanism it follows that:

- $\delta_{w_{q_1}} = \delta \varphi_{\rm{S}_1 \rm{S}_2}$
- $\delta_{w_{\rm{S}_1}} = 2 \cdot \delta \varphi_{\rm{S}_1 \rm{S}_2}$
- $\delta_{w_{q_2}} = \delta \varphi_{\rm{S}_1 \rm{S}_2}$
- $\delta \varphi_{\rm{S}_1 \rm{S}_2} = 0.5 \cdot \delta \varphi_{\rm{S}_1 \rm{S}_2}$

Applying the principle of virtual work gives:

$$
\begin{align*}
\delta A &= 0 \\
-108 \cdot \delta \varphi_{\rm{S}_1 \rm{S}_2} + q \cdot 2 \cdot \delta \varphi_{\rm{S}_2 \rm{S}_3} + q \cdot 4 \cdot \delta \varphi_{\rm{S}_1 \rm{S}_2} &= 0 \\
q &= \frac{108}{6} = 18 \, \rm{kN/m}
\end{align*}
$$

So this is must be the moment distribution at the end of the elastic phase and the hinges in $\rm{B}$ and $\rm{C}$ are reached simultaneously.

From an incremental approach the same conclusion can be drawn.
