:::::{admonition} Exercise
:class: exercise

Determine the plastic normal force and plastic bending moment capacity of the cross-section.

:::::

::::{admonition} Worked-out solution
:class: solution, dropdown

For the normal force capacity:

$$
A = 300 \cdot \sqrt{2} \cdot 450 \cdot \sqrt{2} \cdot \frac{1}{2} = 135000 \, \rm{mm}^2
$$ 

$$
N_{\rm{p}} = f_y \cdot A = 235 \cdot 135000 = 31725000 \, \rm{N} = 31.725 \, \rm{MN}
$$

For the bending moment capacity, we should consider the stress distribution with half of the cross-section in tension and half in compression. The plastic neutral axis is therefore located at the centroid of the cross-section, which is located at $h/3$ from the top of the cross-section:

```{figure} ./2026_06_17_data/stresses.svg
:align: center
:source: https://github.com/Structural-Mechanics-CEG/mechanics-figures-source/tree/main/exam_plasticity_2
:number:
```

The centroids of both halves can be found as follows:

$$ z_{\rm{centroid,1}} = 450\cdot \sqrt{2} - 450 - \cfrac{1}{3} \cfrac{300 + 2 \cdot 300 \cdot \sqrt{2}}{300 + 300 \cdot \sqrt{2}} \cdot \left( 450\cdot \sqrt{2} - 450 \right) \approx 87.9 \, \rm{mm} $$

$$ z_{\rm{centroid,2}} = \cfrac{1}{3} \cdot 450 = 150 \, \rm{mm} $$

This gives:

$$
M_{\rm{p}} = 235 \cdot \cfrac{135000}{2} \cdot \left(450 \cdot \sqrt{2} - 450 - 87.9 + 150 \right) \approx 3942 \, \rm{kNm}
$$

:::::{admonition} Exercise
:class: exercise

Determine the collapse load $F$ for the given structure using collapse mechanisms. Show the governing collapse mechanism.

:::::

::::{admonition} Worked-out solution
:class: solution, dropdown

There are two potential collapse mechanisms, the first one:

```{figure} ./2026_06_17_data/mechanism_1.svg
:align: center
:source: https://github.com/Structural-Mechanics-CEG/mechanics-figures-source/tree/main/exam_plasticity_2
:number:
```

From the shape of the mechanism it follows that:

- $ \delta \varphi_{\rm{AD}} = \frac{1}{2} \delta w$
- $ \delta \varphi_{\rm{DE}} = \frac{1}{2} \delta w$

Applying the principle of virtual work gives:

$$
\begin{align*}
\delta A &= 0 \\
F \cdot \delta w - 300 \cdot \frac{1}{2} \delta w - 300 \cdot \frac{1}{2} \delta w - 300 \cdot \frac{1}{2} \delta w &= 0 \\
F &= 450 \, \rm{kN}
\end{align*}
$$

The second potential mechanism is:

```{figure} ./2026_06_17_data/mechanism_2.svg
:align: center
:source: https://github.com/Structural-Mechanics-CEG/mechanics-figures-source/tree/main/exam_plasticity_2
:number:
```

From the shape of the mechanism it follows that:

- $ \delta \varphi_{\rm{AE}} = \frac{1}{2} \delta w_{\rm{D}}$
- $ \delta w_{\rm{E}} = 2 \delta w_{\rm{D}}$
- $ \delta \varphi_{\rm{BE}} = \frac{1}{3} \delta w_{\rm{D}}$

Applying the principle of virtual work gives:

$$
\begin{align*}
\delta A &= 0 \\
F \cdot \delta w_{\rm{D}} - 300 \cdot \frac{1}{2} \delta w_{\rm{D}} - 40 \cdot 2 \delta w_{\rm{D}} - 300 \cdot \frac{1}{3} \delta w_{\rm{D}} - 300 \cdot \frac{1}{3} \delta w_{\rm{D}} &= 0 \\
F &= 430 \, \rm{kN}
\end{align*}
$$

This mechanisms has the lowest collapse load and is therefore the governing mechanism.
